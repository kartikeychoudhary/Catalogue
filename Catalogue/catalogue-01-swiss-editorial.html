Catalogue nº 01 / Style A

Swiss Editorial
Minimal.

A typographic discipline first, an interface second. We compose with a stable grid, hairline rules, and a single hot accent that earns its weight by being almost the only color in the room. The result is calm, dense, and unmistakably authored — pages read like a quarterly journal that happens to ship as software.

Best used for
Reading apps · Financial dashboards · Long-form publishing
Signature visual move
Oversized italic numerals against a 12-column rule grid
Voice
Edited. Confident. Quiet.

"The grid is the moral compass; the accent is the punctuation."

— House Style, ¶ 2.1

Issue 17 · Spring 2026 · Out May 15

A quarterly letter on typography, archives, and quiet software.

One serif, one grotesque, one accent. Twelve essays four times a year, printed in Zürich and mailed flat. The web edition is a faithful companion, not a substitute.

FIG. 17 / 2026

Patrons

01
A grid you can feel

Twelve columns, hairline rules, generous gutters. The page breathes the same on a phone as on a printed spread.

02
Type that earns its weight

One serif voice, one grotesque, one mono for receipts. No display fonts, no novelty, no shouting.

03
A single hot accent

Used sparingly, for primary actions and the occasional editorial italic. The accent is the punctuation.

04
Density by craft

Tables, archives, and references designed for the long read, not the scroll. Nothing important is hidden behind a click.

05
Light & dark, both intentional

Dark mode is its own issue, not an invert. Tones, paper, and contrast tuned for evening reading.

06
Mobile is the same letter

The grid collapses but the rhythm holds. Tables stack, modals become sheets, and the rule never breaks.
